2001: A Space Odyssey

Overlaps with other films

Overlaps with Robot Dreams
  • Both films are praised for their realism. 2001 is praised for their depiction of the future and depth in special effects, but Robot dreams is praised for emotional depth and relatability.
  • In both films, you have to infer many aspects, due to the very sparce dialogue. You must try much harder when reading 2001: A Space Odyssey, despite it containing more dialogue, because of it's external story.
  • These two films contain much more story outside of the focus of technology compared to the third film. Dave Bowman from 2001 outgrows technology with spiritual evolution, and the monoliths surrounding the universe are being sought after by america's government.
    Robot dreams has plenty of focus with Dog's external interactions with other personalities, and his life without Robot.
  • Recurring musical themes are present in both films. 2001 features recurrent songs that play during the most important scenes.
Overlaps with The Mitchels vs The Machines
  • AI is an antagonistic force in both films. In 2001, it is significantly more subtle, with HAL being suspected of malfunctioning, then slowly killing the crew, turning off life support systems for the unconscious members and removing the atmosphere of the ship and killing Frank.
    In The Mitchels vs The Machines, AI is always a conspicuous threat, capturing all humans in sight and planning to send them all out to space, killing them.
  • Special effects are a focus in both films. In 2001, this is used to enhance immersion and present Stanley Kubrick's vision of the future.
    In The Mitchels vs The Machines, special effects are mostly brought up and are aware by Katie, who is implied to have added these effects themselves for comedy.
Depiction of relationship between humans and AI
  • In 2001: A Space Odyssey, the sole representative of AI is HAL 9000, who is a tragic tale of a self-preserving machine with conflicting programming, who killed the crew due to this.
  • HAL is depicted as a talking, thinking machine with similarities to modern applications such as Zoom and Stockfish, used for faraway contact and recreation respectively.
